This artifact is a project I did working with the company Torchfire on making concept art for a game they are designing. These concepts depict dirt and mud huts covered in moss and have various objects around them depicting the professions of the people that lived in them. The project gave me lots of experience with creating concept art and images as well as actual company experience signing an NDA with them and communicating regularly to identify what the company was looking for.




Artifact Two:

This is an animation that I made using Adobe animate. For this project, we were assigned with making a simple few second animation using some of the simple techniques of animation in our design such as squshing elemts or bending them etc. I had got some pictues of Pikachu online along with the Pokemon Platinum logo and then with some applications of the squash and translation techniques I was able to make what you see here. It was my first attempt at doing animation or really anything art related so it not amazing or anything but it was a start to understanding how I can make pictures of the same drawing interact with eachother to create a design that is unique to the way I create things. I didn't make any of the images or the logos myself on this assignment but I learned how to use an animation program to create effects and actions that other animators use in other shows and cartoons.

Artifact Three:

This is an AR shooter game in which there is an alien invasion coming from another world through portals and your job to to denfend your home and collect candies before the aliens destroy your home and take over the world. It was a group project where I worked with three other people to create an AR game with none of us having much knowledge or experience before getting together. I had a large focus in the art, animations, and particle effects and had given assistance on the AR and programming side of the project as well. This was a fun project to do with our group and we had fun with our idea and made ourselves laugh the whole time as we were putting it together and for my first AR game I am pretty glad at how it turned out.
